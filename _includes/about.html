About Me

I'm a sophomore pursuing a BSBA at Washington University in St. Louis with majors in Economics & Strategy and Mathematics, along with a minor in Computer Science. I am excited by opportunities where my academic interests converge and thus find a variety of fields in business extremely appealing. For example, some industries I am interested in include algorithmic trading, high frequency trading, information technology, accounting, consulting, private equity and venture capital.

To put my interests broadly, I enjoy the process of dissecting complex and ambiguous problems and building elegant and novel solutions. I love any work with this process at its core, whether it's computer programming, data science, entrepreneurship, or anything else. I am always willing to explore new and exciting applications and projects, regardless of how difficult they may be.

I have experience with a few different programming languages and tools. As far as languages go, I am proficient with Python, Java, and C. In addition, I have moderate experience with R, SAS, Assembly, Arduino, HTML and CSS. The tools I am familiar with are Git, Apache SVN, MySQL, Jekyll, and some applications in the Adobe Creative Cloud suite, including Dreamweaver, Illustrator and InDesign.

When I'm not working, I enjoy road trips and cruises with my family and friends. I like watching Rick and Morty, It's Always Sunny, and Silicon Valley in my downtime as well. In addition, I love supporting the Bengals, Browns, Cavaliers, and other Ohio sports teams through the good and (usually) bad times.
